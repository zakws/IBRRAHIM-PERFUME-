# Google listing — setup pack (online-only business)

Two things make IBRAHIM show up on Google. Do both. Neither needs coding.

## 1. Google Business Profile (the map/brand card)
Because there is no physical shop, set it up as an **online business without a public address**.

1. Sign in with the business Google account at business.google.com → **Add business**.
2. Fill in exactly:
   - **Business name:** IBRAHIM Fragrances
   - **Category:** Perfume store (add "Cosmetics store" as secondary)
   - **"Do you want to add a location customers can visit?"** → **No**
   - **Service area:** Australia (or Sydney + major cities)
   - **Phone / website:** the business phone and the live site address
3. Paste this description (750-character limit, this fits):
   > IBRAHIM Fragrances is a Sydney fragrance house with five distinctive eau de parfums: Sultan Oud (dark amber), Glamorous (rose gold), Blue Chill (ocean fresh), Charizma (warm amber) and Magic Caramel (rich amber gourmand). Every 100 mL bottle is $89.99 AUD, with bundle savings, a $45 Discovery Set that is fully redeemable against a full bottle, and free shipping over $150. Designed and shipped from Sydney, Australia.
4. Upload 5-10 photos: use the bottle images from `assets/images/products/` (the `-front-card.jpg` files) and lifestyle shots.
5. Verify the business when Google asks (usually video or phone). Post the Eid offer as a **Google Post** during the campaign.

## 2. Google Search Console + free shopping listings
1. At search.google.com/search-console, add the live site address and verify.
2. Submit the sitemap: enter `sitemap.xml` → Submit. (The site already generates it.)
3. Optional but powerful: at merchants.google.com create a **Merchant Center** account and upload
   `marketing/feeds/google-merchant.csv` → the five fragrances appear in Google's free Shopping tab.

The site itself is already prepared: titles, descriptions, product schema, sitemap and robots are all in place.
